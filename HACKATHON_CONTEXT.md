## Purpose

Participants form teams, build a product or prototype from scratch, and pitch at the end. The event supports technical and non-technical builders, including founders, designers, business students, marketers, engineers, and people interested in startups, AI, and product building.

The event encourages use of Lovable and natural language prompts to build apps, websites, prototypes, landing pages, internal tools, AI products, marketplaces, or similar products. Participants receive 100 Lovable credits, equivalent to a Lovable Pro plan.

## Prize Pool

January Capital reserves the right to withhold remuneration beyond the advertised $1,000 prize pool.

- First place: $600
- Second place: $300
- Third place: $100

Prize winnings are distributed by bank transfer and split evenly across team members independently.

## Pitch Deck

- Pitch decks must be submitted as `.pptx`.
- Deadline: 3:00 PM sharp on May 23, 2026.
- Late submissions will not be accepted.
- Teams with late decks must pitch without visual aid.

## Judging

- Judging decisions are final and non-negotiable.
- Judging criteria were to be explained on May 22, 2026 and emailed on May 23, 2026.

## Work Commencement

Participants must not begin work on their solution before the official hackathon commencement. Early work may result in disqualification at the discretion of judges or organisers.

## Eligibility And Team Composition

- All participants must be registered.
- All participants must attend a portion of the event before pitching to remain prize eligible.
- Each team must contain at least one current UQ student.

## Intellectual Property

Participants retain ownership of IP created during the event. By participating, teams grant UQIES a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use submitted materials for promotional or internal evaluation purposes.

## Conduct And Ethics

Participants are expected to maintain a respectful and inclusive environment. Disruptive, discriminatory, or unethical behavior may result in removal from the event and disqualification.

Projects and submissions must comply with ethical standards. Prohibited content includes:

- Hateful, discriminatory, or harassment-promoting content based on protected characteristics
- Dangerous or harmful content that could reasonably cause physical or psychological harm
- Illegal content or content promoting illegal activities
- Sexually explicit or inappropriate content
- Misinformation or malicious content

The hackathon allows "stupid" and useless ideas, but submissions must remain respectful and safe. Organizers may disqualify teams that violate these guidelines.

## Refunds

Refunds are not issued once the event has commenced, except under exceptional circumstances assessed case by case.

## Photography And Media

Attendees consent to being photographed, filmed, or recorded for promotional purposes by UQIES and partner organizations. Participants who do not wish to appear in media should notify organizers on arrival.

## Modifications

UQIES reserves the right to modify terms, schedule, or venue arrangements as necessary. Significant changes will be communicated via registered contact information.
